Learning-Based Multi-Terrain Robot Locomotion

Institution: UC Berkeley
Timeline: Sep 2025 – Present
Status: In Progress

Training a Unitree G1 humanoid robot to walk across varied terrains using PPO-based reinforcement learning in MuJoCo Playground. Exploring terrain-adaptive locomotion policies and the foundations for sim-to-real transfer.

Problem & Motivation

Humanoid robots need to traverse diverse terrain — flat ground, slopes, stairs, rough surfaces — without hand-tuned gaits for each surface. Reinforcement learning offers a path to policies that generalize across terrain types, but training stable bipedal locomotion is notoriously difficult due to the high dimensionality of humanoid dynamics and the narrow stability margins.

This project is exploring how PPO-based training in simulation can produce robust walking policies for the Unitree G1 across multiple terrain conditions.

Approach

Using MuJoCo Playground as the simulation environment, the G1 humanoid model is trained with Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) to develop walking gaits. The training curriculum exposes the policy to varied terrain conditions to encourage robust generalization.

This project is deepening my understanding of reinforcement learning for physical systems, reward engineering for locomotion, and the practical challenges of training high-dimensional control policies.
